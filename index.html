06Do Filipinos need a visa for Vietnam? What are the entry requirements?+
Filipino passport holders are currently visa-free for short stays in Vietnam.

However, starting April 15, 2026, all visitors must complete a Pre-Arrival Information Submission online before entering Vietnam:
  • Submit at least 3 days before arrival at prearrival.immigration.gov.vn
  • Airport QR code is available but NOT recommended — causes delays
  • All passengers must register at etravel.gov.ph within 72 hours before PH departure
For non-Filipino passports — Visa Upon Arrival (VOA): Passport + Printed Letter of Approval (LOA) + 2 pcs 2x2 white background photo. Fee: USD 40/pax. Regular processing: 7 weekdays. Expedited: 2–5 weekdays (+USD 30). Visa processing closes 1 day before departure — no late applications.
Always verify the latest visa policy per passport type before confirming any booking.

09What makes Ba Na Hills special? Why is it the highlight of the trip?+
Ba Na Hills is a French colonial mountain resort at 1,487 meters elevation — accessible via the world's longest single-line cable car (5,801m, ~20 min ride). At the summit: French Village, gardens, game zones, restaurants, and the iconic Golden Bridge (Buddha Hand Bridge) — giant stone hands holding a golden pedestrian bridge above the clouds. Temperature is 8–10°C cooler than the city.
Remind clients to bring a light jacket — the summit is noticeably cooler. Show photos of the Golden Bridge for instant client interest.
10What is Hoi An and why do clients love it?+
Hoi An Ancient Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site 30km from Da Nang — one of the best-preserved trading ports in Southeast Asia. Famous for colorful silk lanterns lighting the streets every night, the Japanese Covered Bridge (18th century), tailor shops (custom clothes in 24 hours), and riverside restaurants. November has the Hoi An Lantern Festival (full moon night) — extremely atmospheric.

05What is the Sapporo Snow Festival and why is it the centerpiece?+
The Sapporo Snow Festival (Yuki Matsuri) is one of Japan's most iconic winter events, held every February. Features:
  • Enormous, intricately carved ice and snow sculptures — some as tall as buildings
  • Multiple venues across Sapporo — Odori Park, Susukino ice sculptures
  • Spectacular nightly illumination of sculptures (most beautiful 6–10 PM)
  • International sculpting competition + food stalls + entertainment
  • Attracts 2 million+ visitors annually from worldwide
Day 6 is specifically kept free so clients can fully immerse themselves. This is the #1 reason this package exists.
Show clients photos and video of the illuminated ice sculptures at night — it almost always closes the sale immediately.
06What is the unlimited King Crab dinner at Nanda Crab Buffet?+
On Day 5 evening in Sapporo, clients dine at the Nanda Crab Buffet — unlimited Hokkaido King Crab included in the package price. Hokkaido is famous for producing some of the world's finest, sweetest king crabs — the cold Sea of Japan waters produce exceptionally large, premium crabs. "Unlimited" means clients eat as much as they want: steamed, grilled, as sashimi, in soups.
This dinner is consistently rated as the top highlight of the trip by returning Hokkaido travelers. Show the photo of the king crab spread — the visual is irresistible and almost always seals the deal.
07What is the Penguin Parade at Asahiyama Zoo?+
Asahiyama Zoo in Asahikawa hosts a famous Penguin Parade — a daily winter event where penguins walk along a snow path through the zoo grounds with zoo keepers. Completely free with zoo entrance. Only held during winter months (December–March). The penguins waddling in a line through the snow is irresistibly cute and universally loved — great for families with children and anyone who loves animals.
Find the Asahiyama Zoo Penguin Parade video on YouTube and share it in your group chats. 30 seconds of penguins in the snow is one of the most effective recruitment tools for this package.

10What should clients pack for Hokkaido in February?+
Hokkaido in February: 0°C to –10°C (or colder with wind chill). This is extreme cold — do NOT let clients underpack.
Mandatory: Heavy down coat (windproof, waterproof) · Thermal underlayers top & bottom (at least 2 sets) · Waterproof snow boots (ankle-high or higher) · Thermal gloves (not fashion gloves!) · Warm scarf + thermal beanie · Hand warmers (kairo packs — buy in Japan 7-Eleven, ¥100 each) · Extra pairs of thermal socks.
Good news: JAL's 2×23kg baggage means clients can pack all winter gear without baggage fee stress. This is a selling point — emphasize it.
11Is skiing at Rusutsu included? What should clients prepare?+
Rusutsu Ski Resort is visited on Day 4. Ski suits and equipment are NOT included — clients must rent on-site. Rusutsu is one of Hokkaido's premier ski resorts, known for exceptional powder snow (Hokkaido powder is legendary among skiers — consistently rated the best dry powder in Japan).

Clients who want to ski: budget for equipment rental on-site. Clients who don't ski can enjoy the resort scenery, observation decks, and relaxation facilities.
12What is Otaru and the Shiroi Koibito Park?+
Otaru is a historic port city near Sapporo, visited on Day 7 — last sightseeing day before the airport.
  • Otaru Canal: Gas lamp-lit historic canal with stone warehouses — beautiful with snow on the banks. Classic Hokkaido winter photo spot.
  • Kitaichi Glass: Handcrafted Venetian-style glasswork — great for unique souvenir shopping
  • Music Box Museum: Thousands of music boxes — whimsical and very Instagrammable
  • Shiroi Koibito Park: Theme park built around Hokkaido's most famous cookie (Shiroi Koibito — white chocolate wafer sandwich). Includes a factory tour and a massive shop. One of the best pasalubong stops in Japan — the cookies are extremely popular as gifts.
Shiroi Koibito cookies are one of Japan's most popular omiyage (souvenir) items. The 2×23kg JAL baggage is perfect for stocking up here.
13What is Noboribetsu Date Jidaimura (Hell Valley area)?+
Noboribetsu Date Jidaimura is a samurai-era heritage theme village — streets and buildings recreated from Japan's Edo period (1603–1868). Clients can watch ninja performances, samurai demonstrations, and explore the historic atmosphere in full costume recreations.

Jigokudani (Hell Valley) just below is one of Hokkaido's most dramatic landscapes — volcanic steam shooting from the earth, boiling orange pools, sulfurous atmosphere. The "Hell Valley" name comes from the fiery, otherworldly appearance. A completely unique Japan experience far outside the usual Tokyo-Osaka circuit.
